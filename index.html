Traditional Shakshuka

Difficulty: Easy

5 out of 5 stars

Shakshuka is a delicious dish typically served in the Middle Eastern and North African regions. Its main components are that of eggs, tomatoes, garlic, onions, and a myriad of spices. This dish provides the perfect start to the day with its rich flavors. Many have created variations of the meal, yet remain true to its base ingredients. Not only is it a healthy and nutritious meal, but it is also fairly easy to make! Serve it for one, or serve it for many; its the perfect dish to be served in one pan.

Cooking Information:

  • PREP TIME: 10 mins
  • COOK TIME: 20 mins
  • TOTAL TIME: 30 mins
  • YIELD: 4-6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • 1 28-ounce can whole peeled tomatoes
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt, plus more as needed
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper, plus more as needed
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 small bunch fresh cilantro, chopped, for serving
  • 1 small bunch fresh parsley, chopped, for serving
  • Hot sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  • Heat olive oil in a large sauté pan on medium heat. Add the chopped bell pepper and onion and cook for 5 minutes or until the onion becomes translucent.
  • Add garlic and spices and cook an additional minute.
  • Pour the can of tomatoes and juice into the pan and break down the tomatoes using a large spoon. Season with salt and pepper and bring the sauce to a simmer.
  • Use your large spoon to make small wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into each well. Cover the pan and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the eggs are done to your liking.
  • Garnish with chopped cilantro and parsley.

    • ****If you are not dairy free, crumbled feta or goat cheese on top is delicious addition. This dish is usually served with toasted pita bread, but it also paired well with avocados. Be sure to also use a stainless steel pan versus a cast iron pan, since tomatoes are acidic and could erode the seasoning of the cast iron pan; it may also bring out a metallic flavor.
